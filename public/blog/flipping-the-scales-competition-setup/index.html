How to Setup a Competition on Flipping The Scales

Just Four Choices and a Start Date

Posted by Ryan on January 10, 2019

I’m so happy that you’ve decided to join with your friends and family to lose weight as a group. For years my family and I have been hosting these types of competitions every so often - but we did it the old fashioned way, with a notebook and a pencil. I’ve built flippingthescales.com to eliminate that hassle.

In order to setup a competition just click on the 'Create Competition' link in the navigation bar. Note that you must be logged in to see the link.

When you setup a competition you’ll need to make 4 choices about the structure of the competition.

Entry Fee - Ok, this one is self explanatory. This is the amount that every participant needs to pay to enter the competition. The entry fee drives the overall prize pool. For each participant that enters the competition the prize pool is increased by their entry fee. For example, if 10 participants enter a competition with a $50 entry fee the total prize pool will be $500.

Entry Fee - The payout structure drives how the winner (or winners) of the competition will be paid. Three options are available:

  1. 100% of the prize pool to 1st place
  2. 75% of the prize pool to 1st place and 25% to 2nd place
  3. 60% of the prize pool to 1st, 25% to 2nd, and 10% to 3rd place

Interim Prizes - A weight loss competition can get boring if everyone is just battling for the final prizes. Because of that I’ve introduced the option for interim prizes into the competition. Interim prizers are are paid to the participant with the highest percentage lost over a shorter time period (2 to 4 weeks).

When selecting an interim prize you can either choose a payout of 5% of the total prize pool or 10% for each of the interim awards. If you choose to use interim awards in your competition these awards will be deducted from the prize pool that is available for distribution at the end of the competition.

Competition Length - The final selection that needs to be made is then length of the competition. Flipping the Scales supports competitions that are between 8 and 20 weeks.

In addition to the four choices you’ll need to make about the structure of the competition, the competition creation screen also requires that you name your competition, select a start date, and gives you the opportunity to automatically send out email invitations to those that you would like to join the competition.

Good luck to you, I hope you lose tons of weight, and if you have any questions or problems you can reach me at admin@flippingthescales.com.
